## Research

### Projects

#### [Identities from Random Matrices](https://mxm.math.wisc.edu/spring-2026/#identities-from-random-matrices)
Spencer Venancio, [Shenqi Qiu](https://dvk0213.github.io/), Lingfang Yuan <br>
**Advisor**: [Benedek Valk&oacute;](https://people.math.wisc.edu/~valko/); **Graduate Student Mentor**: [Yahui Qu](https://yahiuqu.github.io/)


Our project extends recent work by our advisors on the sine-$\beta$ process, a one-parameter family of point processes arising in random matrix theory. In their preprint, they derive the pair correlation function (which captures the likelihood of finding points in two given regions) for $\beta$ as a $2n$-th order ODE. We pursued two extensions of this result. On the computational side, we wrote a Mathematica program that outputs these ODEs for arbitrary $n$. On the mathematical side, we focused on the case $\beta = 6$, where we aimed to replace the classical formula, in terms of a sixfold integral, with a nicer expression. We did this by decomposing the third-order complex ODE as a first-order ODE composed with a second-order one, then solving them in sequence. This part of the project is ongoing.
